    • About "Villeta, Cundinamarca"

    Villeta, is a Colombian town located in the department of Cundinamarca, about two hours from Bogotá. It has warm, mountain weather. It is known for its sugarcane production and is one of the main panela centers in the country. In the past, Villeta was an agricultural town, and now it is a popular tourist destination.

    About the festival

    The Panela Festival celebrates the tradition of panela production in Colombia. It is a cultural event with parades, contests, music, and exhibitions. The festival has been celebrated since 1977. During the event, farmers show how panela is made. Last year, more than 20 towns participated.

    1977: The first Panela Festival was held in Villeta. It was created to celebrate the panela industry and the work of local farmers.
    1980s: Traditional cane grinding demonstrations were added, along with beauty contests and folk music shows.
    1990s: The festival became popular nationwide. Visitors started coming from all over Colombia.
    Between 2000 and 2010: More cultural activities were included, such as dance performances, parades, and art exhibitions. The festival grew in size and importance.
    Between 2011 and 2019: The festival continued growing with more visitors, better organization, and media coverage. It became one of the most important local festivals in Cundinamarca.
    2021: The festival was canceled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
    Today: The festival is active again. It includes cultural, musical, and sports activities, and attracts many national and international tourists.
    • Villeta produces more than 10% of Colombia's panela.
    • During the festival, the National Panela Queen is chosen.
    • Farmers do live demonstrations of the panela-making process.
    • The festival includes traditional sports like "tejo".
    • Panela is one of the most representative products of rural Colombian culture.
